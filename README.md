## About the Data

The dataset utilized in this dashboard originates from clinical birth records in Georgia, specifically focusing on maternal outcomes in the Atlanta metropolitan area. It comprises a sample of several thousand birth encounters collected between 2020 and 2022 to investigate racial disparities in birth outcomes. The data was systematically compiled from hospital electronic health records and includes diverse maternal cohorts categorized by socioeconomic status and clinical risk factors. The study population specifically targets maternal-infant pairs with recorded geographic coordinates and complete clinical histories. All individual-level data has been de-identified to maintain patient confidentiality and adhere strictly to medical research ethics.

## Project Importance

This dashboard identifies high-risk geographic clusters and clinical disparities in preterm births across Georgia, providing actionable spatial insights for healthcare providers to target interventions. By visualizing the intersection of socioeconomic status and maternal health outcomes, it supports evidence-based policymaking aimed at reducing racial inequities in infant health.
